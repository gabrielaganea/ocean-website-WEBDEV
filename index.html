Save our oceans



The Earth's Oceans are threatened everyday. More and more sea creatures become extinct or endangered. Sea levels are rising and the temperature keeps increasing causing coral bleaching.

Approximately 90% of its predatory fish population it's lost. More than 8 million tons of plastic is being discarded in the water. The Ocean is changing faster than at any time in the last 65 million years.










Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and contain approximately 97% of Earth's water.

Separated by continents and island chains, the oceans are divided into five: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern (Antarctic), and Arctic.

What's the most polluted Ocean?

Every area of the ocean is polluted, and according to a study made by the Ocean Cleanup Foundation, even though there is also glass and wood in the sea, 99.9% is plastic.

Till today, in many parts of the world, sewage water is discharged in the ocean - untreated or under-treated. This can cause serious effect on marine and human life and can also lead to eutrophication. Sewage leads to the decomposition of organic matter that in turn leads to a change in biodiversity. Even if the ocean's ecosystem isn't destroyed entirely, it is still changed drastically, and usually not for the better.
