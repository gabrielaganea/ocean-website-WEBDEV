The first step in protecting the Oceans is to learn about them. Discover what makes them so amazing and how they influence our lives. Harming them means harming us!









Fact #1

The deepest known area of the Earth's oceans is known as the Mariana Trench. Located in the western Pacific Ocean, it measures about 2,550 km in length and 69 km in width. It's maximum depth point is almost 11 km.

The Mariana Trench has also been touched by pollution. Researcher found extremely elevated concentrations of PCBs and that amphibods ingest microplastics. In 2019, a plastic bag and candy wrappers have been discovered at the bottom of the trench. It has also been reported that carbon-14 from nuclear bomb testing has been found in the body of aquatic animals.

Fact #2

The Pacific Ocean is larger than the Moon. It might sound crazy, but it's true! As the largest Ocean on Earth, the Pacific contains around 25,000 islands and its diameter is 5 times wider than the Moon.

It's no wonder that the Pacific is the most polluted Ocean, with no less that 2 trillion plastic pieces. There is an island of garbage twice the size of Texas inside the Pacific Ocean: the North Pacific Gyre off the coast of California is the largest oceanic garbage site in the entire world.

The Ocean is beautiful, intriguing, mysterious. It has so many amazing places from an underwater waterfall, to the deepest canions. Sunken historic artefacts form underwater museums. The largest mountain range is found under the ocean. The Mid-Oceanic Ridge, stretching over 56,000km, is a mountain chain that runs along the centre of the ocean basins.

The Ocean is also protecting us. It absorbs quantities of heat reaching us from the Sun, stores them and releases them slowly. The heat is distributed around the world by ocean currents, shaping the climate of different continents. The ocean is also absorbing one third of the carbon dioxide emissions we produce, serving as a buffer for climate change impacts.

Food and fresh water also comes from the Ocean. Fish and seafood are the main source of protein for four out of ten people. From the ocean surface, water evaporates and rises as vapor into the atmosphere, where, upon colliding with colder air, it forms clouds and rain, providing us with irrigation and drinking water.












Ocean tides are caused by the Earth rotating while the Moon and Sun's gravitational pull acts on ocean water.

Aproximately 90% of world trade is carried out by sea.

The majority of life on Earth, approximately 94%, lives under the oceans.

About 70% of the oxygen we breathe is produced by the oceans.
